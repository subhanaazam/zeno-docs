# Knowledge Notes

::: info Free Version
This page covers the Free Version. [View Pro docs →](/settings/data-source/knowledge-notes)
:::

The **Knowledge Notes** tab lets you add custom written notes directly to your chatbot's knowledge base — without needing to publish them as pages, posts, or PDFs. This is perfect for facts, policies, FAQs, or internal knowledge that you want your chatbot to know but don't want to display publicly on your site.

## Adding Knowledge Notes

![Knowledge notes tab](/images/free/page72_1.png)

The **Knowledge Notes** section is where you write notes that will be used as additional AI context. The description reads *"Add markdown or plain text notes. They are used as additional AI context."*

The tab is split into two panels side by side: an **Editor** on the left and a **Preview** on the right.

### Editor

![Editor panel](/images/free/page73_1.png)

- **Editor** — Type your notes in the left-hand text area. You can use either **markdown** formatting or **plain text**, whichever you prefer. The placeholder inside the editor reminds you: *"Type markdown or plain text here..."* and a helper note below confirms *"Markdown and plain text are supported."*

::: tip
Use headings, lists, and bold text in markdown to structure your notes clearly — this helps the AI understand and retrieve the right information when answering questions.
:::

### Preview

The right-hand panel shows a **live rendered preview** of your notes. If you're writing in markdown, this is where you'll see how the formatting looks once it's rendered. This is purely for your reference as you write.

## Managing Your Notes

![Clear and build index buttons](/images/free/page74_1.png)

At the top-right of the Knowledge Notes tab, you'll find two action buttons:

- **Clear** — Click this button to remove all of your Knowledge Notes and start fresh.
- **Build Index** — Click this button to save your notes and add them to the chatbot's knowledge base. Your chatbot will then be able to use these notes when answering questions.

That's it! You now have a lightweight way to teach your chatbot anything — no new pages, no uploads, just notes.
